It's funny though, cause I enjoyed watching his review of the film more than the film itself. Anyways, I really enjoyed Ebert's analysis of films. It wasn't just entertainment but an art form and a rich medium of communication. I wasn't that big a fan of Siskel though. I never thought he was as insightful as Ebert.

  • The Up Series

    This is a documentary series which follows the lives of a dozen or so British children from age 7 til 56. As such, it is the longest spanning documentary series I know of. Namely, the first release was in the 1950s when they were age 7 and the last release was in the 2000s, when they were 56. By following these individuals through most of their lives through one of the most tumultuous periods in human history, we gain a penetrating into glimpse into the modern human condition. That alone is why this is probably the most important film every made. My wife's coworker told us about this movie and just from the premise alone I knew I had to watch it and it did not dissapoint.
